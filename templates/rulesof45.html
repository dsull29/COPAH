The Rules of 45

Objective:Partnerships try to win books and prevent their opponents from doing so.

Players:Four or six, in teams of two with partners in alternating seats.

Deal: Each player is dealt five cards in batches of 3-2 or 2-3.

Bidding: The bidding starts with the player to the dealer's left and continues clockwise. Each player in turn may either bid or pass. Bids are made in multiples of 5, up to 30, with no suit being mentioned starting with 15. In addition there is also the dreaded 30 No Draw, which means the bidder's team will make all the books without allowing anyone to draw additional cards from the deck. Each bid must be higher than the preceding bid, except that the dealer in his turn can take the bid as his bid. This means that he bids the same as the last bid; he has the right to name the trump if no one bids higher than he. After the dealer takes the bid. The winning bidder names the trump suit.

Drawing: Each player discards face down as many cards as he wishes from his hand. The dealer then restores the player's hands to five cards, starting with the player to his left. (Variant: permit the dealer to "rob the pack" to replace his discards. This means he looks through the remainder and picks out whichever cards he wants in his hand. This is only done in 6 man when the cards run out.)

Play: The player to the left of the dealer makes the opening lead. The hand is played out in books. If unable to follow the suit led, any card may be played. A book is won by the highest trump played on it, or the highest card of the suit led if no trump are present.

A hand must follow suit to a lead, if able, with the following exceptions:

(a) Even though able to follow to a non-trump lead, a player may trump if he pleases.

(b) The three highest trumps (5, J, Aâ™¡) have the privilege or reneging when a lower trump is led. For example, if the trump six is led, a player holding any of these three top trumps without lower trumps may discard instead of following suit (if he has lower trump also, he must play a trump). But there is no reneging against a higher trump. For example, if the trump jack is played, the holder of the five may renege but not the holder of the Aâ™¡.

Scoring: Each book counts 1, and the highest trump in play counts as 1 book. After the hand, each side counts what they have taken. If the bidder's side has taken at least the amount of the bid, they score all they have won. If this side fails, the amount of the bid is deducted from its score. The other side in either case always scores what it has won in books. If a side bids and makes 6 (all the books), they score 9, as in 9 hole. This may sound a little funny and it is. A 30 No Draw wins if completed gets 12 points. If they get set on a 30 No Draw they go back 9.

Game: The team to first reach a total of 31 points and be ahead by two or more points wins the game. If a team gets -31 they have fallen out the back door and consequently have lost.

Misdeal: There must be a new deal by the same dealer: (a)if he deals any hand a wrong number of cards, and it is caught before there has been a bid; (b) if he fails to adhere to his chosen dealing plan; or (c) if a card is exposed during the deal.
