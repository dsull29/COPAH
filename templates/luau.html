Pictures: Luau 2002
A'ohe hana nui ka alu'ia.
Translation: "No task is too big when done together."
     It was the best of times; it was the worst of times. Ok the 2002 COPAH Luau wasn't that epic but no doubt I'll be telling my grand-kids about it someday. If only to explain how I got skin cancer. Next time you see a fat Irish guy outside in the sweltering sun drunk and shirtless tie him down and put some SPF 2000 on him. He'll thank you later. I really shouldn't complain. What kind of moron complains about beautiful weather? Temps were in the 90's with almost no clouds in Norton that day. Which meant great conditions for being outside drinking beer, eating some BBQ, and chatting with old friends.
     Chef Tom worked the grill. Serving up his world-famous chicken kabobs, hamburgers, hot dogs, and even a turkey burger or two. Many guests also brought deserts and other dishes. From what I remember all the food was great. Tom's chicken kabobs were so good you had to wait in a line at the grill if you wanted one.
     We also played some sports. Volley and wiffle ball were heated battles. Most of the volleyball battle was limited to just setting up the damn net though. Wiffle ball went on until well after the sun had set. Oakley was of course the all star here. Nobody has made an art of pitching a wiffle ball quite like Oakley.
     Of course there was the drinking. Phipps provided two kegs, one Miller Lite and one Bud Lite. Becky created Tiki Gods to go over the kegs to add to the Luau theme. Tom also rigged up a mini water cooler filled with kamikaze shots, which were strong, a lot too strong. With all this drinking surely there was wacky hi jinx happening. Over the course of the day people were thrown into the pool. This wasn't too big a deal since these people were mostly standing on the pool deck in a bathing suit, but when Moles picked up Becky and threw her in the pool from the ground outside the pool. Well I don't think she was too happy.
